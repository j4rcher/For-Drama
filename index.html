Punchdrunk Gecko Paper Birds

Punchdrunk was formed in 2000 by artistic director Felix Barrett. They focus on “blending classic text, physical performance, award-winning design installations and unexpected sites”.
Their style of theatre is heavily immersive (related to Promenade Theatre); the audience is free to choose whether to focus on individual performances and themes, or “treat [the performance] as an art installation.”
They practice “location theatre” often performing in outside, open spaces. Unlike Gecko and Paper Birds, they mix both classic plays and devised work.
Recently, they’ve begun wearing Greek masks during performances as they found that “suddenly inhibition fell away and people found a sense of freedom in their anonymity”.
Their ultimate aim is to reject “passive obedience” to the production that is usually expected of audiences. This is where they get their name; they want the audiences to feel “Punchdrunk” after performances.
They are a registered charity, and often run workshops in schools and colleges.
